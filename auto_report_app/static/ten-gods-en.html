Ten Gods · 十神

Ten relationships between you and the world

The Ten Gods are derived from how the other stems and elements relate to the Day Master. They describe how you express, learn, compete, earn, carry responsibility, and form close relationships.

How to read them

Start with which signals are strongest, where they sit, and whether combinations, clashes, major luck, or annual timing activate them. No Ten God should be read in isolation.

Ming Atelier Note

Inner roles, not fixed labels

A strong signal can become either an advantage or a source of friction. The deep report places each Ten God back into its pillar, hidden stem, branch relationship, and timing context before drawing a practical conclusion.

The Ten Roles

Chinese technical names are retained for accuracy. The English descriptions explain how each role tends to appear in real decisions and relationships.

比
比肩 · Friend

The Independent

Friend represents self-direction, equal peers, personal standards, and the need to act on your own judgment.

  • Strength: steady self-belief and peer collaboration.
  • Shadow: rigidity, comparison, and difficulty sharing control.
劫
劫财 · Rob Wealth

The Challenger

Rob Wealth represents competition, courage, resource exchange, partnership, and the pressure created when money and peers become entangled.

  • Strength: speed, resilience, and market instinct.
  • Shadow: impulsive splits, unclear ownership, and rivalry.
食
食神 · Eating God

The Maker

Eating God represents craft, output, pleasure, teaching, care, and the ability to turn skill into something useful and repeatable.

  • Strength: patient creation and sustainable delivery.
  • Shadow: comfort seeking, delay, and avoiding hard decisions.
伤
伤官 · Hurting Officer

The Edge

Hurting Officer represents expression, critique, innovation, persuasion, and the urge to challenge rules that feel inefficient.

  • Strength: originality, communication, and breakthrough thinking.
  • Shadow: conflict with authority, overstatement, and sharpness.
财
正财 · Direct Wealth

The Operator

Direct Wealth represents earned income, budgeting, stable clients, practical responsibility, and consistent value exchange.

  • Strength: discipline, reliability, and cash-flow awareness.
  • Shadow: overwork, narrow security thinking, and carrying too much.
偏
偏财 · Indirect Wealth

The Opportunity Hunter

Indirect Wealth represents commercial instinct, networks, flexible resources, project income, and opportunities outside a fixed salary path.

  • Strength: negotiation, reach, and resource integration.
  • Shadow: overextension, speculation, and unstable attention.
官
正官 · Direct Officer

The Steward

Direct Officer represents rules, reputation, duty, professional standards, institutions, and the ability to carry visible responsibility.

  • Strength: credibility, order, and dependable leadership.
  • Shadow: pressure, perfectionism, and fear of making mistakes.
杀
七杀 · Seven Killings

The Fighter

Seven Killings represents urgency, competition, authority pressure, decisive action, and the need to perform under demanding conditions.

  • Strength: courage, strategic response, and crisis leadership.
  • Shadow: chronic pressure, conflict, and acting before support is ready.
印
正印 · Direct Resource

The Protector

Direct Resource represents education, credentials, mentors, protection, research, and systems that restore confidence and capacity.

  • Strength: learning, depth, and reliable support.
  • Shadow: dependence, hesitation, and excessive preparation.
枭
偏印 · Indirect Resource

The Mystic Thinker

Indirect Resource represents unconventional insight, specialist knowledge, intuition, strategy, and the ability to study what others overlook.

  • Strength: pattern recognition, originality, and independent research.
  • Shadow: isolation, suspicion, and difficulty translating insight into action.
Ten Gods support self-understanding. A full reading must also consider structure, branch relations, ShenSha, major luck, and annual timing.
